Asteroid Information

An asteroid is a small, rocky object that orbits the Sun. They are remnants from the early solar system, approximately 4.6 billion years old.

Location

  • Asteroid Belt: Region between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter where most asteroids are found.

    • Photo of Asteroid Belt:

  • Near-Earth Asteroids (NEAs): Asteroids that orbit close to Earth and can pose impact risks.

    • Photo of Asteroid near Earth

  • Trojan Asteroids: Asteroids that share orbits with planets, commonly found near Jupiter's Lagrange points.

    • Photo of Trojan Asteroid:

Size and Shape

  • Asteroids range in size from tiny pebbles to hundreds of kilometers in diameter.
  • They are often irregularly shaped due to insufficient gravity to form spherical bodies.

Composition

  • C-Type (Carbonaceous): Dark, carbon-rich, and the most common type (~75%).
  • S-Type (Silicaceous): Silicate rocks and metal (~17%).
  • M-Type (Metallic): Contain large amounts of metals like iron and nickel.

Notable Asteroids

  • Ceres: Largest asteroid, about 940 km in diameter, classified as a dwarf planet.

Asteroids and Earth

  • Asteroids have struck Earth in the past, like the one that caused the extinction of dinosaurs 66 million years ago.
  • Monitored Near-Earth Objects (NEOs) assess potential threats to Earth.

Space Missions

  • OSIRIS-REx (NASA): Collected samples from asteroid Bennu.
  • Hayabusa2 (JAXA): Brought samples back from Ryugu.
  • DART Mission: Successfully tested asteroid deflection technology for planetary defense.

Interesting Facts

  • Asteroids are sometimes called minor planets.
  • Some asteroids have small moons or form binary systems.
  • Asteroids hold clues about the formation of the solar system.
