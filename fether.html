Exoplanets Information

What Are Exoplanets?

Exoplanets are planets that orbit stars outside our solar system. Since the first discovery in 1992, thousands have been identified, with many more awaiting confirmation. They vary widely in size, composition, and orbital characteristics.

Characteristics of Exoplanet Types

  • Gas Giants: Composed of hydrogen and helium.
  • Neptune-like: Often icy with thick atmospheres.
  • Rocky Planets: Found in a star's habitable zone.
  • Super-Earths: Can vary widely in composition.
  • Rogue Planets: Travel through interstellar space.

How Do We Detect Exoplanets?

  • Transit Method: Observes a star's brightness.
  • Radial Velocity (Doppler Shift): Detects a star’s wobble.
  • Direct Imaging: Captures pictures of planets.
  • Microlensing: Relies on gravitational bending of light.

Interesting Exoplanets

  • Kepler-22b: A super-Earth in the habitable zone.
  • TRAPPIST-1 System: Seven Earth-sized planets.
  • TOI-700d: Earth-like planet in a habitable zone.
