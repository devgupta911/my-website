Comet Information

A comet is a small celestial body made up of ice, dust, and rocky materials. It orbits the Sun in an elongated, elliptical path and develops a glowing coma and tail when it approaches the Sun.

Composition

  • Nucleus: The solid core made up of ice, dust, and rocky material.

  • Coma: A cloud of gas and dust surrounding the nucleus, formed as the comet heats up near the Sun.

  • Tails: Comets develop two types of tails:
    • Dust Tail: Made of small dust particles pushed away by sunlight.

    • Ion Tail: Made of charged particles (ions) pushed by the solar wind.

Orbits and Origin

  • Comets typically have elongated, elliptical orbits that bring them close to and far from the Sun.
  • They are believed to originate from:
    • Kuiper Belt: A region beyond Neptune containing icy bodies.

      • Photo of Kuiper Belt:

    • Oort Cloud: A distant spherical shell of icy objects surrounding the solar system.

  • Halley's Comet: The most famous comet, visible from Earth every 76 years. Last seen in 1986.

Interesting Facts

  • Comets are often referred to as "dirty snowballs" because of their icy and dusty composition.
  • The tail of a comet always points away from the Sun due to the pressure of solar wind.
  • As comets pass close to the Sun, they lose material, which can eventually cause them to disintegrate.

Space Missions

  • Rosetta Mission (ESA): Studied Comet 67P/Churyumov-Gerasimenko and landed the Philae probe on its surface.
  • Stardust Mission (NASA): Collected samples from Comet Wild 2.
  • Deep Impact (NASA): Studied Comet Tempel 1 by crashing a probe into its surface.

Differences Between Comets and Asteroids

  • Comets are made primarily of ice and dust, while asteroids are mostly rocky and metallic.
  • Comets form tails when near the Sun, whereas asteroids do not.
